Challenge 5: Accessibility Concerns

 Much of the internet presents challenges to those with visual impairments, as it is predominantly reliant on sight.
In Challenge 2, there is a very low contrast ratio between the chess pieces and their matching colored space. A screen reader
might also struggle identifying the unicode tags and telling the listener where a piece is within the board. This can be fixed in
part by selecting different colors than black and white as the cell backgrounds.

 Navigating a website can also be difficult, which is why we use large headers to signal where content is. This challenge is also
helped using a Table of Contents and hyperlinks. However, one oversight with this page is there is no way to get from one section
to the other easily without going back to the Navigation Bar. This could be fixed with a bar that follows the user down the page
to allow them to navigate more easily.
